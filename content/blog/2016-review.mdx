Every year, I conduct my [Annual﻿ Re﻿view](https://www.cloudliving.com/?s=annual+review) and share it publicly.

I find it helpful for me to reflect on my successes and failures of the previous 12 months, but also I hope you can learn something or be inspired reading my reviews as well.

This annual review is more detailed than my previous ones as I’ll look back at what happened in the 9 most important areas of my life last year by answering the following 3 questions:

1.  What went well this year?
2.  What didn't go so well this year?
3.  What am I working toward?

Let's get into the details...


### Health & Fitness

What Went Well?

-   Maintained a good health throughout 2016 without any major sickness
-   Consistently worked out 3 to 5 times per week
-   Ate clean and healthy very consistently (5 to 6 times per week)
-   Got stronger and bigger than I was in 2015 and that’s thanks to the [Kinobody’s Greek God program](http://my.kinobody.com/p/greek-god-program)

What Didn’t Go Well?

-   Progressed slowly with my key lifts due to my constant neck and lower back pain problems
-   Still had a hard time following my macros in order to shred some fat and achieve the lean physique that I want

What I’m Working Toward?

-   I decided to take a break from weightlifting as it seemed to be the cause of my neck and lower back problems.
-   I’m currently working on improving my flexibility, mobility, core strength and straight arm strength by following GMB’s programs ([Elements](https://gmb.io/e/) and [P1](http://gmb.io/p1/)).


Headstand at Mui Ne's beach.

### Mind & Emotions

What Went Well?

-   As I got older, I noticed a lot of changes in terms of beliefs and perspective.
-   I thought about deaths a lot and knowing that me or my loved ones can leave this world anytime soon makes me a much more responsible person.
-   I actively spent more time with my family and close friends.
-   I realized that I need a lot less to be happy and that I actually have everything I need to be happy now.
-   One person that heavily influenced my way of thinking is [Derek Sivers](https://sivers.org/). I’m a big fan of his work and his approach to life and business. I actually had a chance to briefly meet him in person at DCBKK 2014.
-   Here are some of my most favorite pieces of content from Derek:

-   [Anything You Want](https://www.amazon.com/Anything-You-Want-Lessons-Entrepreneur/dp/1511366079/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&linkCode=ll1&tag=cljblo-20&linkId=c9a2c3ccfc72f51536289ecdc85ab195) (book and audiobook) - I've re-listened to the audio version of this book more than 40+ times.
-   Derek Sivers interviews on Tim Ferris’ podcast (EP [#125](http://fourhourworkweek.com/2015/12/14/derek-sivers-on-developing-confidence-finding-happiness-and-saying-no-to-millions/), [#128](http://fourhourworkweek.com/2015/12/28/derek-sivers-reloaded-on-success-habits-and-billionaires-with-perfect-abs/) and [#202](http://fourhourworkweek.com/2016/11/21/tools-of-titans-derek-sivers-distilled/))
-   [Derek’s 2015 talk at World Domination Summit](https://sivers.org/wds)

-   Overall, I’m calmer and happier than I’ve ever been.

What Didn’t Go Well?

-   There were still some moments when I felt stressed and unmotivated to do anything, but that’s normal. Everybody has low times.

What I’m Working Toward?

-   Meditation helps a lot when it comes to training the mind to be calm and happy so I’ll try to maintain a daily meditation practice with the help of the [Headspace app.](https://www.headspace.com)
