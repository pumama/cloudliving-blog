## What Exactly is Cloudways?

CloudWays is a managed cloud hosting platform that allows you to deploy multiple applications including WordPress, Drupal, Joomla, Magento and more in just one click.

Unlike other managed hosting providers, they actually don’t operate any physical servers themselves.

Instead, they provide an intuitive user interface that lets you deploy and manage servers from the top 5 cloud providers, such as:

-   Digital Ocean
-   Vultr
-   Linode
-   Google Cloud Platform
-   Amazon Web Services

Pricing is a little complicated, as it depends both on the server spec you need and the cloud provider you choose.


You’ll see that Cloudways’ pricing is higher than the listed prices on any of the providers’ websites because they add a management fee on top of the base prices.

That also covers for customer support via 24/7 live chat and ticket for any hosting-related issues.

Servers deployed on Cloudways are automatically configured with the most optimized technology stack to provide you with the best speed, performance & security.

That means you wouldn’t need to spend time learning to manage servers or hire a developer to help manage your servers for you.

All that time saved could be spent on actually growing your business instead.

## What I Like about Cloudways

### The Best Performance You Could Possibly Get

When your sites are hosted on your own cloud server, you’ll not have to share resources with anyone else.

That means better & more reliable performance.

You also won’t have any similar limitations that most shared hosting will put on your account.

For example, Siteground has [quite low CPU limits](https://www.shoutmeloud.com/siteground-hosting-cons.html) for their hosting plans and once you reach the limit you would either have to upgrade or their system would automatically take your site offline until the issue is resolved.


### Affordable Pricing That Fits Everyone’s Budget

Cloudways has different pricing plans depending on the cloud server providers and sever specs you choose.

The higher the specs, the more you pay.

This is great because you only pay for what you need.

My recommendation is to go with Linode since they’ve consistently been showing the best performance during my tests.


**Google Cloud Platform** and **Amazon Web Services** are a lot more expensive and don’t seem to provide as much added benefits compared to Linode.

**Digital Ocean** has always been the slowest cloud provider so it doesn’t get my recommendation.

If you’re starting out, the $12 / mo Linode plan would certainly be enough. But as you grow, you might need to switch to higher plans.


The great benefit of using a cloud server is that you can set up as many sites as you want on your account. But as your traffic increases, you need to pay attention to your **IDLE CPU percentage**.


If it is consistently below 20%, then that’s when you should upgrade to a stronger server.

Cloudways would also notify you via email when this happened:


Scaling a server is as easy as dragging the slider to the right and click “Scale Now”. It would take only 5 to 15 minutes to complete the process with no downtime on your website.


[Click here](https://support.cloudways.com/how-to-increase-server-resources/) to read their documentation on scaling server size.

### Intuitive User Interface

Cloudways has done a great job at making a simple and easy to use admin panel.

They don’t have too many confusing options like CPANEL (it’s what most hosting companies use) and only feature what you need to deploy and manage your servers/applications.

After signing up for a trial account and logging in, you’ll be prompted to deploy your first server.


Just fill in the required information, choose the smallest Linode plan, pick the location closest to your audience, and click “Launch Now”.

I’ll take approximately 7 minutes for your server to be ready.


### Launching a New WordPress Website In Just a Few Clicks

A default WordPress installation is already created when you deploy your server.

However, if you want to add more. It’s very easy!

Just visit **Applications** from the menu bar, then click on **Add Application**


Select WordPress from the first dropdown, name your app, select the project this new application belongs to and click “Add Application” one more time.


In just one minute, your WordPress installation will be ready.


Now click on Domain Management, enter your domain in the field and click “Save Changes”.

Now all you need to do is to point your domain to your server’s IP address and your site will be live!

[Here’s a tutorial](https://support.cloudways.com/how-do-i-take-my-website-live-from-cloudways/#tab-2) on how to do it.

### 1-Click Let’s Encrypt SSL Setup

Setting up SSL for your website is very easy with Cloudways. Just visit “SSL Certificate”, type in your email and domain name, then click **Install Certificate**


### Free WordPress Caching Plugin


When you launch a WordPress application using Cloudways, they’ll automatically install their own caching plugin on your website called [Breeze](https://wordpress.org/plugins/breeze/).

It’s fairly simple to configure and can be a good alternative to [W3 Total Cache](https://wordpress.org/plugins/w3-total-cache/) or [WP Rocket](https://wp-rocket.me/).

I tested it on a few sites and the results look comparable to the 2 plugins mentioned above.


### Migrating a WordPress Website to Your Server Has Never Been Easier


Here comes the part I love the most about switching to Cloudways.

They have made the migration process incredibly simple and easy.

Just install this [Cloudways WordPress Migrator](https://wordpress.org/plugins/bv-cloudways-automated-migration) on your website, fill in your new application details, click Migrate and you’re done!


Your WordPress site will be migrated over to Cloudways very quickly.

This really saves you a lot of time and money as you no longer have to wait for your developers to do the migration for you.

### 1-Click Site Staging

A staging environment is used when you want to create a clone of your site for testing and development.

Once the staging environment is set up, you can push and pull changes to test the application.

With Cloudways, you can set this up in just one click.


For more information about this feature, [click here](https://support.cloudways.com/create-staging-environment-site/)

### Automatic Backup & Restore

This is a pretty standard feature that all hosting providers should have. So there’s really not much to talk about this.

You can set up automatic backup or take an on-demand backup inside your Server Management panel.

Cloudways also has a documentation on this [here.](https://support.cloudways.com/how-to-backup-my-server/)

### Easy Team Collaboration

If you have a team then this is a really easy way to give them controlled access to your Cloudways account. More on this feature [here](https://support.cloudways.com/how-to-create-and-update-team-members/).
